Upcoming Projects

I am in the process of doing some research that will allow me to get started on a personal project I find very interesting. While on vacation recently, it was getting dark and I had the privilege of watching some bats fly around. I took note of the fact that they fly in a seemingly very chaotic manner, and remembered that they use ultrasonic echolocation to navigate in the dark and detect their surroundings. This chaotic flight behavior is actually indicative of their extreme agility and amazing sensory capabilities. With my understanding of sound, and newly gained backend skills, I started to think about creating something that could mimic this ability.

The first phase of my project will involve sound localization. I would like to train an AI equipped with a microphone array by emitting sounds from speakers that are surrounding the mics. After succesfully being able to identify direction and approximate distance, I would like to create a new iteration of the robot that incorporates 5 ultrasonic speakers and microphones. The robot will emit high frequency (30kHz-250kHz) bursts that are followed by silent intervals to analyze the reflections. The burst/silence interval will likely be dynamic, and dependenton the distance from an object and it's speed. These bursts and silences in bats tend to be anywhere from 5-10 miliseconds, to 100 milliseconds. This is extremely fast, and leads me to think about the amazing processing speed of the auditory system in bats. Not only is the speed impressive, but they are also so accurate in analyzing the reflections of the high frequency sounds they produce, that they can identify something as small as human hair. AI echolocation is an incredibly fascinating prospect. The applications are immense. This could allow a robot to, "see," around corners and be a failsafe for computer vision if a robot's vision is lost. By using frequencies in the megahertz range, a robot could also potentially see through objects or containers, and know the contents of them - something akin to x-ray vision.
